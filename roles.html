Manifesto

I guess anyone shapes a set of principles and believes over the time. They are most often unwritten. May be some people find writing them down stupid. In my view making them explicit increase your chance to keep these principles and adapt them.

My manifesto is a compilation from other peoples thoughts. Check the slideshow bellow to see what is all about.




1. Focus.

There are no stupid people.
There are two types of people - those that can focus and those that can not.
Learn how to focus in a world full of distractions with Leo Babauta.

2. Then Reinvent Yourself.

Know that now you have more chance than ever to make a difference, to spread your ideas, and to make an impact.
Seth Godin will teach you how to connect, be generous, make art, aknowledge the lizard, ship, fail, and learn.

3. Energize Yourself Daily And Be Proactive.

If you are proactive the good things will happen.
Proactivity requires energy. How to obtain more energy?
Altucher's daily practice for your physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual shape could do the trick.

4. Create things lead by a principle.

Create things but preferably towards solving particular problem (even if it is only in your head).
Otherwise your actions are unnecessary and could be better for you to do nothing.
So find a guiding principle that shapes your decisions in a problem solving process and create something useful.
See example how to do this in the amazing talk by Bret Victor.

5. Don't be afraid of failure.

Sometimes you have all ingredients to do something great but the fear of failure is your stopper.
Check Seth Godin's "Poke The Box" and you will probably stop doing it anymore.

6. Do what you love to do but be aware about the sex and cache theory.

Hugh MacLeod explains that in most of the cases you are going to need a job that pays the bills.

7. The system is not as important as your contribution to it.

Probably you are going to need a job that pays the bils. However, dream jobs are most often created than found.
So if it happens to be part of a system - not created by you - working from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. don't complain about that.
Don't be mediocre in anything you do. Be not easily replaceable. Be a Linchpin

8. And finally...don't push your knowledge to others. Spread it on demand only.

Apart from my personal experience there are many evidences that we must avoid giving advices before being asked.
1. People are not always ready to hear what you have to say according to 4 stages of competence model.
2. Tao Te Ching (Laozi) praises self-gained knowledge. Layers of interpretation and subjectivity must be removed.
3. As Christensen says: “Questions are places in your mind where answers fit. If you haven’t asked the question, the answer has nowhere to go...."

Father

Ok. We have a set of principles and believes that we follow. I wrote that we don't have to push our knowledge to others. In my view your own children are exception to this rule.

When you become father you become a manager....you become a coach. The children are your best players. They are motivated, they are listening to you, they copy you, they admire you.....they are ready to bring your knowledge into the next level. They can leverage your principles and shape them in a way that could make the world a better place to live in.

So take your chance and watch them playing now! Play with them now! Don't wait until they grow up! It will be too late.
