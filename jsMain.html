Building Applications With JavaScript Technology Stack

Last Update: Saturday, October 20, 2012
Tweet
Lately, I am interested in learning how to develop applications with JavaScript-only technology stack. Back in 2011 I wrote why I would like to spend time on JavaScript. This is actually kind of ordered list with links to other people thoughts and why they invest time in JavaScript. So, these were enough for me and I was in. But when I started I found that the language, or more precisely the ecosystem around it, is full of new technologies, libraries, frameworks, standards etc. It was kind of a jungle to me.

I know that there are many books out there that cover the JavaScript language, or particular library or framework. But I wanted to have something like a map that is more oriented towards the development process. For example, I needed to know which technology, framework, aspect, or technique fits where in the development process, or in which part of a layered software architecture.

So I allowed myself to create a sample content (categories or chapters if you want) that will help me organize my knowledge. There are a lot better resources on the web about this kind of stuff. But I am using this classification since it fits well my needs. Each category has a summary with short explanation what it is all about and links to resources that I found most useful for understanding particular topic. As part of each category I am going to publish more detailed blog posts for more concrete stuff. These blog posts are often heavily influenced by materials on the web or sometimes evolved as a result of my practice with related technologies.

As you can see this is a work in progress. However, my wish is to cover most of the things in a period of 1 year. So I plan to update this content very often.

If you like you could follow me on twitter to get notifications for the latest updates.
